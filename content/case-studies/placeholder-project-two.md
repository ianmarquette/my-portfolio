## Problem

Four product teams were maintaining their own component libraries, leading to inconsistent UIs, duplicated effort, and a growing gap between design and engineering implementations.

## Process

We audited 1,200+ UI components across the four codebases, identified 47 unique patterns, and ran a series of working sessions with engineers and designers from each team to align on a shared vocabulary.

The resulting design system used a token-based approach with a clear contribution model so teams could extend the system without forking it.

## Outcomes

Component duplication dropped by 60%. New feature development time decreased by 30% in the first quarter after rollout. All four teams adopted the system within six months.
